There is either a 1 or a 0 behind the question mark (?). We will choose one of your independent decisions randomly after you have completed the experiment and calculate your payoff based on this decision round.

Your payoff depends either on whether there is a 1 behind the question mark (‘lottery group’) or on your assessment of the probability that a 1 is hidden behind the question mark (‘assessment group’). Whether you are in the lottery group or in the assessment group will be determined randomly and with equal probability.

If you are in the lottery group, you obtain $5 if there is a 1 behind the question mark (?) and $2.5 if there is a 0 behind the question mark (?).

If you are in the assessment group, your payoff depends on your assessment. Your payoff is between $2.5 and $5 and is calculated in such a way that you receive the maximal expected payoff if you state your true assessment of the probability.

Details concerning your payoff:

If you are randomly assigned to the lottery group, you obtain $5 if there is a 1 behind the question mark (?) and $2.5 if there is a 0 behind the question mark (?).

If you are assigned to the assessment group instead, your payoff is calculated according to the following rule: Let q be your stated assessment of the probability that a 1 is hidden behind the question mark (?). After you have stated q, the true value behind the question mark (?) is used to determine your payoff. Should there in fact be a 1 hidden behind the question mark (?), you receive a payoff of $5 with a probability of 1-(1-q)*(1-q) and a payoff of $2.5 otherwise. Should there be a 0 hiding instead, you receive $5 with a probability of 1-q*q and $2.5 otherwise.

In addition to your assessment of the probability, we will ask you after every round to state how certain you are that your assessment is correct.
